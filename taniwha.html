Taniwha

Taniwha are supernatural creatures in Maori tradition, similar to dragons and serpents in other cultures. They were said to have lived in the ocean, caves, and lakes.
Some taniwha would kill people, or kidnap women. Others were believed to be guardians for a tribe, and people would give them presents and say a karakia (a spell).

Taniwha looked like giant lizards, and some of them had wings. Other ones were reptile-like sea creatures. Taniwha could take the shape of whales, sharks and even logs of wood in rivers.
